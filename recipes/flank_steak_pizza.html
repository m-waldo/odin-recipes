Flank steak pizza (or: Matambre a la pizza)

What about this mouthwatering dish?

Back in Argentina we have a great devotion to pizza, but also to meat. It was just a matter of time until someone combined both culinary experiences into one!

This recipe is great if you are inviting friends or family over and want to surprise them with a really flavorful meal. follow these steps if you want to really please their taste buds.

Ingredients

To prepare the steak (ahead of time)

  • 2 lb of flank steak, pounded to 1/2 inch thick
  • Salt and pepper
  • 1 cup of milk

For The Sauce

  • 1 tsp of olive oil
  • 1 small onion, minced
  • 1 large garlic clove, minced
  • 1 can of crushed tomatoes
  • 1 tsp of oregano
  • 1 tsp of paprika
  • 1/4 tsp of crushed red pepper flakes
  • 1/4 tsp of salt

For putting it all together

  • 4 oz of mozzarella cheese (sliced or shredded)
  • 1-2 small tomatoes, sliced (optional)
  • Roasted red pepper (optional)
  • Sliced deli ham (optional)
  • Green olives (optional)
  • Oregano (to top)

How to prepare it

The steak

  1. Place the pounded steak into a large baking dish. Season the steak with salt and pepper and cover it with milk. (Add a little extra milk if the steak is not completely covered.)
  2. Cover the dish and refrigerate for 8-12 hours.

The sauce

  1. Heat the oil in a medium saucepan over medium-high heat. Add the minced onion and garlic, and saute until softened, about 5 minutes.
  2. Reduce the heat to medium and add the tomatoes, oregano, paprika, chile flakes, and salt. Bring the sauce to a simmer. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the sauce has thickened, 10-12 minutes.

Putting It All Together

  1. Remove the marinating meat from the refrigerator 1 hour before you want to grill it, to let it come to room temperature.
  2. Preheat grill to a medium-high heat.
  3. Drain the milk from the steak, discarding the milk. Pat the steak dry.
  4. Place the steak on your preheated grill and grill the first side for 5-7 minutes, uncovered.
  5. Flip the flank steak.
  6. Spread the cooked side with your prepared pizza sauce. Spread the mozzarella cheese over the sauce, and top with the tomatoes (and other toppings if desired).
  7. Cover the grill and cook for an additional 3-5 minutes, until the steak is done and the cheese has melted.
  8. Remove the steak from the grill and let it stand for 5 minutes before slicing, to let the juices settle.
  9. Garnish the matambre a la pizza with oregano to serve (if desired). Serve with roasted (or grilled) potatoes and grilled bread.
